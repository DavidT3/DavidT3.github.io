Dr David J Turner

| Research Associate | Michigan State University | turne540@msu.edu |

I am an expert in X-ray astronomy and clusters of galaxies, using clusters as laboratories to explore various aspects of astrophysics and cosmology. My research is enabled by the open-source Python X-ray astronomy modules XGA and DAXA; I am the founder and lead developer, managing the addition of new features. This is part of my mission to democratise astronomy specialisms through easy to use software.

Ongoing Research Projects

X-ray Local Volume Complete Cluster Survey (X-LoVoCCS)

I am the X-ray lead for the LoVoCCS collaboration, using archival X-ray observations to probe the the ICM (measuring temperature, metallicity, gas/total mass, etc.), complementing the extraordinary 2D weak-lensing mass maps being measured. This project involves making the X-ray analysis entirely reproducible and open; read more in the the LoVoCCS collaboration repository.

Hydrostatic Masses of SDSS Selected Galaxy Clusters

This involves the measurement of hydrostatic masses (and thus temperature and hot baryon density radial profiles) for ~120 galaxy clusters observed by the XMM-Newton X-ray telescope, but selected from the SDSS redMaPPer catalogue. These clusters are a subset of the SDSSRM-XCS sample created by Paul Giles for XCS. This project makes use of X-ray: Generate and Analyse (XGA).

XMM Analysis of LOFAR (LoLSS and LoTSS) detected radio sources

I am leveraging XGA's ability to easily analyse X-ray data for large samples to produce an X-ray property catalogue for LOFAR selected objects, with upper-limits for non-detections. It will be reproducible as XGA is open-source, and a 'cookbook' for analysing LOFAR sources with XGA will be included. This catalogue is intended as a resource for the community, with myraid scientific uses.

Open Source Astrophysics Software

X-ray: Generate and Analyse (XGA)

Open source Python package for general X-ray astronomy analysis.

Democratising Archival X-ray Astronomy (DAXA)

Open source Python package for the creation of multi-mission X-ray archives, for use with XGA and other analysis software. Complete sets of X-ray observations from multiple telescopes can be downloaded and processed, or only observations relevant to a user's work.
